Bitcoin Address Visualization

Understanding the Graph

The graph on the right visualizes Bitcoin transactions associated with the wallet address 12h8BbuTYYC6zWpTNfbYoGv4LpVGv38MUx.

This wallet address is represented by a big red circle in the graph.

What Do the Nodes and Edges Represent?

  • Nodes: Represent individual Bitcoin wallet addresses.
  • Edges: Show transactions between two addresses.

Node Colors

Red Nodes: Indicate accounts with zero balance.

Green Nodes: Indicate accounts that still hold some balance.

Interactive Features

  • Hovering: Hover over any node (circle) to display the Bitcoin address it represents.
  • Zoom Controls: Use the zoom in (+) and zoom out (-) buttons in the bottom-right corner of the graph to navigate and analyze dense transaction networks.

Why Is This Important?

Such analysis can significantly enhance the ability of authorities to monitor and track the flow of funds between Bitcoin accounts.

Example Insight:

From observation, some accounts send money as soon as they receive it. These red bitcoin accounts could be categorized as shell accounts.

Note: This cannot be confirmed with certainty but can serve as a basis for further investigation.
